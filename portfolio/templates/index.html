About Me

Diplomacy

Jane Njuguna is a powerful global voice for youth inclusion, climate action, global health, and sustainable development. She is a trained climate negotiator, an experienced public speaker, and a sustainability influencer passionate about driving real-world impact through diplomacy, storytelling, and youth-led innovation.

She has worked with the Ministry of Foreign and Diaspora Affairs (MFDA) of Kenya, contributing to bilateral diplomacy initiatives including the planning of Kenya’s historic state visit to the United States, the first by a sitting African president since 2008.

Climate Leadership

Jane is actively engaged in multilateral spaces, having participated in the UNDP Climate Security Academy, where she explored the intersections of climate, peace, and youth-led action. Her training also includes the Climate Governance, Diplomacy, Leadership, and Negotiations Programme by the African Group of Negotiators Experts Support (AGNES) and UNICEF’s Introduction to Thinking and Working Politically course.

Additionally, Jane completed the Global Health course by One Young World and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, deepening her understanding of health equity, development systems, and the links between public health and the climate crisis.

Public Speaking

As a public speaker, Jane delivers talks on international platforms from United Nations side events to youth summits using her voice to spotlight marginalized issues and inspire inclusive solutions. As a sustainability influencer, she harnesses the power of digital advocacy to promote climate resilience, mental well-being, and gender equity.

Innovation & Youth

Jane actively collaborates with organizations, institutions, and movements committed to accelerating the SDGs and building a just, climate-resilient future. She is always open to partnerships that prioritize inclusion, innovation, and action, believing that sustainable development can only be achieved through meaningful cooperation and shared vision.

Driven by faith, grounded in service, and fueled by purpose, Jane continues to champion a future where young people are not only heard but are shaping the policies, systems, and conversations that define our world.
